# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

High school and college public forum debaters who want real, ranked rounds without the scheduling overhead of live debate. They're competitive but time-constrained — practice rounds fall apart because calendars never align. They care about their craft, respect the format, and want a ladder that actually means something. They open the app between classes, after dinner, whenever a free window appears.

## Product Purpose

An asynchronous public forum debate platform. Debaters challenge each other, record speeches on their own schedule, receive full judge ballots, and climb an ELO ladder. Success looks like: a competitive student completing more rounds in a semester than they ever could through in-person practice, with feedback that actually improves their game.

## Brand Personality

Warm, collegiate, community-first. The voice is a knowledgeable teammate — confident about the craft, never cold or clinical. Emotional goal: the feeling of a debate team clubhouse where rivals become friends and everyone takes the work seriously.

## Anti-references

- Cold SaaS / startup templates: navy-gradient dashboards, metric-hero layouts, VC-pitch aesthetic
- Esports / gaming dark mode: neon accents, aggressive chrome, dark-by-default because "it's competitive"
- Academic / institutional: university portal dryness, government-grade UI, zero personality

## Design Principles

1. **The clubhouse, not the arena.** Competitive energy lives inside the rounds. The product shell — nav, dashboard, profile — should feel like a warm space where rivals and teammates coexist, not a scoreboard you're always being ranked by.
2. **Craft signals credibility.** Real judged ballots and real ELO earn trust that numbers alone can't. The design reinforces seriousness through precision and care — not flash.
3. **Async as freedom, not compromise.** "Record whenever" is the product's core promise. Every flow should frame schedule flexibility as an advantage, not an apology for not being live.
4. **Legibility before beauty.** Speeches, ballots, resolutions, and feedback are the actual product. Design serves comprehension first; the photobook aesthetic amplifies content, never competes with it.
5. **Warmth through the work.** The literary design language isn't decoration — it signals that debate here is treated as an intellectual art form, which is exactly what draws the student who takes it seriously.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigation for all core flows (challenge, upload, view ballot). Screen reader support for dynamic state changes (round status, ELO updates). `prefers-reduced-motion` honored for all animations.
