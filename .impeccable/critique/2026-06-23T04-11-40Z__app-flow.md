## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Does NOT read as AI-generated. This is an idiosyncratic, domain-true tool (debate "flowing") with custom interactions you can't template: column color-coding, slash-trigger extensions, a derived read-doc with per-card read-time, WPM calibration. No identical card grids, no hero-metric block, no gradient text. The dark glassy chrome wrapping warm paper document surfaces is a genuine, committed point of view. This is the opposite of slop.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs --json app/components/flow app/flow` returned `[]`. Caveat: the detector parses HTML/markup, and this feature is entirely React TSX with `className` strings, so the scan is effectively blind here — a clean result is not evidence of clean markup.

**Visual overlays:** Not available. `/flow` is gated by `FlowGate` (allow-listed accounts) and needs Supabase auth + seeded flow data, so no reliable user-visible overlay was injected.

## Overall Impression

The strongest thing here is also the trap: this is a power tool built for people who already know how to flow a round, and it rewards them richly. But the entire reach layer assumes a mouse and a wide screen. The flow rail — your only way to switch or create flows — lives off-screen at `translateX(-102%)` and only returns on hover. There is no tap, no click, no button. On a phone (which PRODUCT.md says is a primary context: "between classes, after dinner") the core navigation is simply unreachable, and the rest of the workspace has exactly one responsive breakpoint in ~1,000 lines of flow CSS. The biggest opportunity is to make this capable thing reachable.

## What's Working

- **Domain fidelity.** "Flow," "send doc," "read doc," "tags & cites," "AT doc," "prep" — the language is exactly what debaters use. The read-doc's per-card read-time estimate with a "trim ~N words" pace chip is a real insight into the actual job (hitting speech time).
- **The chrome/paper duality.** Dark glass workspace, warm paper documents (`#fffdf8` modals, `#fff` read doc with container queries). It signals "tool vs. artifact" and fits the Cozy Photobook brand without copying the marketing surface.
- **Power-user depth done quietly.** Slash autocomplete mirrored across Flow/Speech/Send, drag-resizable splits with persisted widths, Alt+←/→ flow switching, speech-length presets, hands-free extension shortcuts. The accelerators are genuinely good.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Primary navigation is hover-only and the workspace has no responsive story**
- *Why it matters:* `.flow-rail` is translated off-screen and revealed only by `:hover` on the rail or a left-edge hotzone. Touch devices have no hover, so there is no way to open the flow list, switch flows, or create one (the empty state even instructs "Hover the left edge"). The multi-pane split, pointer-drag resizers, and hover handles also have no small-screen fallback; the only media query in the flow CSS is `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- *Fix:* Add an explicit tap/click toggle for the rail (a persistent hamburger/handle in `.flow-work__head`), make it a slide-over drawer below ~720px, and collapse splits to a single switchable pane on narrow widths. Treat hover reveal as a desktop enhancement, not the mechanism.
- *Suggested command:* `/impeccable adapt`

**[P2] The read-doc toolbar uses light-surface colors on the dark workspace chrome**
- *Why it matters:* `.flow-readstat` (`#6f6557`), `.flow-readview__hint` (`#8a7f6f`), `.flow-pace--live`/`.flow-target b` (`#2a2118`) are warm dark-on-light values, but the bar sits on the dark pane (the read DOC itself is correctly a white card). On the dark chrome these are near-invisible — well under 4.5:1. The word count, read-time estimate, and pace chip are the whole point of that bar.
- *Fix:* Recolor the readview bar with the chrome's white-alpha family (matching `.flow-sendedit__hint`) or give the bar its own surface. Audit muted text generally: `rgba(255,255,255,0.3–0.45)` placeholders/labels/counts fail AA on the dark base.
- *Suggested command:* `/impeccable audit`

**[P2] Discoverability depends on memory, not recognition**
- *Why it matters:* Rename = double-click. Drag handles, the move-to-folder select, delete buttons = hover-only (`opacity: 0`). Switch flows = hover the rail. Extensions = slash triggers. The keyboard-shortcut overlay that documents all of this is itself opened only by an unlabeled "?". A returning debater can learn it; a first-timer sees a near-empty screen.
- *Fix:* Surface a couple of always-visible affordances (a visible "⋯" row menu on items, a labeled "Shortcuts" entry already in the ⋯ menu — good — plus a first-run hint), and keep at least one non-hover path to every destructive/structural action.
- *Suggested command:* `/impeccable onboard`

**[P2] Inconsistencies across the surface**
- *Why it matters:* "aff/neg" in the rail vs "Pro/Con" in the timers mixes debate formats (PF is Pro/Con; aff/neg is LD/Policy) and makes the side coding ambiguous. Share exists as both a dock tab and a modal; Extensions as both dock and full page. ShareDialog and SnippetLibrary use inline `style={{...}}` while everything else is class-driven — drift that will rot.
- *Fix:* Pick one side vocabulary and use it in the rail, dots, and timers. Decide one canonical home per panel. Move inline styles into the `flow-*` class system.
- *Suggested command:* `/impeccable clarify`

**[P3] Destructive actions without a guardrail**
- *Why it matters:* Deleting a flow and clearing a speech both `confirm()` (good), but deleting an extension, removing a collaborator, and deleting a folder happen instantly with no confirm and no undo. Losing an imported AT extension library to a stray "×" is unrecoverable. The native `confirm()` dialogs that do exist also clash with the crafted aesthetic.
- *Fix:* Add confirm or an undo toast to the remaining destructive paths; replace `window.confirm` with an in-brand dialog.
- *Suggested command:* `/impeccable harden`

## Persona Red Flags

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User):** The flow rail is unreachable without hover — Casey cannot open, switch, or create a flow on a phone. Pane splits and drag-resizers assume a pointer. Timer controls are 24×22px and icon buttons are small, all under the 44×44 target. No responsive layout. Casey can't start.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent):** Aff/neg/other status is conveyed by colored dot alone (`--aff`/`--neg`/`--other`) with no text label. Hover-only controls (drag handle, delete, move) have no keyboard-reachable equivalent surfaced. Read-doc bar contrast fails AA. Many muted layers sit at 0.3–0.45 white alpha. The chevron in SnippetLibrary is a `role="button"` span (keyboard-handled — good), but the pattern is uneven.

**Jordan (Confused First-Timer):** Lands in a near-empty dark workspace. The only nav instruction is "hover the left edge." Buttons are glyphs (`＋▢`, `⤢`, `⏱`, `↺`, `⬆`). Slash triggers, double-click rename, and Alt+arrows are invisible. Jordan won't find the flow list.

**Tariq (project persona — time-crunched PF debater, phone-first):** Opens the app between classes to flow a practice round. Wants to thumb through his flows, drop a card, check his read time. Hits a wall immediately: no tappable flow list, no thumb-zone primary action, splits he can't drive. He'd flow on paper instead — exactly the failure the product exists to prevent.

## Minor Observations

- Silent autosave (speech + send doc) gives no "saved" signal; for shared docs a small synced/saving indicator would reassure.
- `flow-rtcal__step` reads as a small-caps eyebrow on every modal state ("Read speed," "All set," "Step 1 of 2"). Functional as step labels, but it is a repeated kicker.
- Timer accent colors are hardcoded inline (`#6f9bea`, `#e0704f`) rather than tokens.
- The 2px tinted `border-left` on `.flow-node` is technically the side-stripe shape, but it is a legitimate, functional indent guide whose fixed hexes intentionally match the clipboard copy color — defensible, not decorative. Leave it.
- `.flow-empty__eyebrow` is uppercase tracked; fine as a single instance.

## Questions to Consider

- If a debater's primary moment is "phone, between classes," what is the one-thumb version of this workspace — and is the split-pane desktop view actually the secondary mode?
- Could every hover-revealed control have a visible resting state without making the workspace feel busy?
- Does the side coding want to be Pro/Con everywhere, given the product is Public Forum?
